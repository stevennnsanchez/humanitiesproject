Editorial Argument

She was not writing for the Bishop.

This apparatus focuses on the opening movement of La Respuesta — pages 153 to 157 — where Sor Juana establishes her rhetorical position before making any direct argument. Our editorial claim is that La Respuesta is not actually a response to the Bishop of Puebla. It is a text written for future readers, and the opening pages are where that becomes most visible.

She writes sixty pages when a contrite reply needed two. She performs humility while citing Saint Thomas and Saint Paul in the same breath — references that only land if your reader is paying close attention. She explains things the Bishop already knows, which only makes sense if she is also talking to someone who does not. She signs her real name to a letter written under a pseudonym. These are not accidents.

Our apparatus is built around making that gap visible: the gap between her stated audience and her real one. The notes track the moments where those two come apart. That gap is the most important thing a reader of this text needs to see.
